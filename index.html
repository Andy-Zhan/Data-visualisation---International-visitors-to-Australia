Foreign Visitors to Australia

Visualising the trends in foreign visitors to Australia throughout the years

Visitors by country of residence

Where do Australia's visitors come from?

The map below shows the countries that visitors originate from. The size of a country's dot represents how many visitors come from that country.

Apart from the sharp drop of over 80 percent due to COVID-19 travel restrictions, the total number of foreign visitors has quite steadily increased over the past three decades, peaking at almost 9.5 million visitors in 2019.

Currently, Australia receives the most visitors from China, and remains an extremely popular destination for New Zealanders. Among the top 10 source countries, the last decade has seen strong growth from Asian countries.

Hover your mouse along the line chart below to explore the trends in the nationalities of foreign visitors over time.

Reasons for visiting

Why do people visit Australia?

The chart below shows the trends in the main reason for visiting as stated by foreign visitors to Australia, normalised by the total number of visits each year.

Holidays have consistently been the most popular reason for visiting Australia, followed by visiting friends and relatives. However, the number of holiday visitors is slowly decreasing, while the number of people visiting relatives is increasing. This is likely due to the increasing numbers of immigrants living in Australia.

Visits by month

When do people prefer to visit Australia?

The chart below shows the popularity of each month for foreign visitors. For each month, this is expressed as the percentage of yearly visitors that visited during that month, aggregated across all the included years.

The chart also shows the average minimum and maximum temperatures for each month. In general, visitors seem to prefer the warmer weather, with December being the most popular month on average, followed by February. However, the increase in visitors during July indicates that Australia may also be a popular destination for Northern Hemispheric visitors to escape the summer heat.

Visitor count and average spend for popular locations

Which locations are most popular for visitors?

The chart below shows, for each year, the number of visitors received by popular locations in Australia, along with the total expenditure of the visitors for each location.

Use the slider below the chart to see how the data has changed over time.

The slope of the dotted line shows the average spend of a visitor that year. Observe how the average spend of visitors generally increases over time. Locations above the line have an above average spend per visitor, and locations below the line have a below average spend per visitor.

Sydney consistently has the most visitors and total expenditure, but Melbourne often has a higher spend per visitor.
